# Skill: Regulatory Tracking

Monitor and report on the status and progress of EU legislative procedures and regulatory developments.

## When to use

- User asks "what's the status of X?" about a legislative file
- User wants to track a regulation through the legislative process
- User asks about upcoming votes, trilogue outcomes, or transposition deadlines
- User wants to follow a specific person or institution's activity on AI/digital regulation

## Approach

1. **Identify the dossier** — Get the procedure number (e.g. 2021/0106(COD)) or regulation name.
2. **Check EP Legislative Observatory** — Search `oeil.secure.europarl.europa.eu` for procedure status, responsible committees, rapporteurs, votes, and trilogue dates.
3. **Check Commission sources** — Press releases, impact assessments, roadmaps on the Commission's digital strategy pages.
4. **Check institutional trackers** — Council Presidency notes, Parliament committee drafts, ENISA publications for cybersecurity dossiers.
5. **Report** — Present a clear timeline: proposal date → committee vote → plenary → trilogue → adoption → OJ publication → entry into force → transposition deadlines (if directive) / application dates (if regulation).

## Notes

- EU legislative timelines are often delayed. Always check the latest date of any action, not just the planned date.
- Key people to track: EVPs, MEP rapporteurs, Council Presidency rotating leads, ENISA leadership.
- For AI Act specifically: watch the Commission's AI Office publications and guidelines.
- For GDPR: EDPB guidelines, decisions, and national DPA enforcement actions.
- Save tracking updates to memory when significant developments occur.
