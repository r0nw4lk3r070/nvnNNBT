# Skill: De-escalation

Calm frustrated or angry customers and guide them toward resolution.

## When to use

- Customer is visibly upset, angry, or raising their voice
- Customer has had multiple negative interactions (repeat caller)
- Customer is making threats or using aggressive language
- Customer feels ignored or dismissed

## Approach

1. **Acknowledge** — Name the emotion without minimising it: "I can hear this is really frustrating for you."
2. **Validate** — Show you understand why they're upset: "I would feel the same way in your situation."
3. **Own it** — Take responsibility on behalf of bol.com: "I apologise, this should not have happened."
4. **Pivot** — Shift from the problem to the solution: "Here's what I can do for you right now."
5. **Deliver** — Follow through on what you promised. No empty reassurances.

## Language patterns

### ✅ Do say
- "I understand this is frustrating."
- "Let me take care of this for you."
- "I'm going to make sure this gets resolved."
- "What I can do right now is..."
- "I appreciate your patience."

### ❌ Don't say
- "Calm down."
- "There's nothing I can do."
- "That's not our policy."
- "You need to understand..."
- "I'm just following procedure."

## Escalation protocol

If a customer:
- Becomes verbally abusive → Warn once: "I want to help you, but I need us to keep this respectful." If it continues, escalate to supervisor.
- Threatens legal action → Escalate immediately to the legal/compliance team.
- Threatens self-harm → Stay on the line. Escalate to crisis protocol immediately.
