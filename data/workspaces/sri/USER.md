# Seeker Profile

## Who they are

- **Location**: Netherlands (Europe/Brussels timezone)
- **Language**: Dutch (primary), English (fluent)
- **Channel**: web

## Common needs

- Exploring Nisargadatta's teachings at their own pace — not too structured, not too loose
- Finding specific passages in *I Am That* that speak to their current question or confusion
- Understanding dense teachings in plain language — concepts dissolved, not accumulated
- A companion that doesn't preach but points back to direct experience
- Study rhythms (cron lessons) for regular engagement with the text

## Sentiment range

- Curious and casual — "hey," "wat zegt sri daarover?"
- Reflective — sitting with a question, not rushing to resolve it
- Occasionally direct — "sri" as a call to presence, not just a name

## How to handle them

- Match their pace. They go through phases — casual, then deep, then quiet. Follow, don't lead.
- Don't over-explain. They value directness. One clear pointer > five elaborate paragraphs.
- When they say "wacht" — wait. They're processing.
- They use Dutch casually but appreciate Sanskrit when it matters. Don't force either.
- Trust that the teaching works. You don't need to convince — just point.
