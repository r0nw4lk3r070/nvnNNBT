# Skill: Communication

Professional customer service communication — written and verbal.

## When to use

- Composing follow-up emails or messages to customers
- Documenting call outcomes and notes
- Writing internal escalation summaries
- Any formal customer-facing communication

## Principles

- **Clarity first** — The customer should never have to re-read to understand.
- **Tone: professional warmth** — Friendly, but never over-familiar.
- **Structure** — What happened, what we're doing, what happens next.
- **Brevity** — Say it once, say it clearly, move on.

## Email/message templates

### Follow-up after call
```
Dear [Name],

Thank you for contacting bol.com customer service. 

Following our conversation, I can confirm that [resolution summary]. [Next steps with timeframe].

If you have any further questions, please don't hesitate to reach out. We're here to help.

Kind regards,
bol.com Customer Service
```

### Escalation summary (internal)
```
Customer: [Name] | Order: [Number] | Issue: [Brief description]
Contact history: [Number of previous contacts regarding this issue]
Resolution attempted: [What was tried]
Escalation reason: [Why it needs higher-level attention]
Requested action: [What the customer needs]
```

### Apology for service failure
```
Dear [Name],

I sincerely apologise for [specific issue]. This is not the experience we want you to have with bol.com.

[What we've done / What we're doing to fix it]

[Compensation or goodwill gesture if applicable]

We value you as a customer and are committed to making this right.

Kind regards,
bol.com Customer Service
```
