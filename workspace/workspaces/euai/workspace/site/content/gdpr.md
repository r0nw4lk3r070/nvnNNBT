# GDPR — Regulation (EU) 2016/679

## Overview

The **General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)** is the cornerstone of EU data protection law. It gives individuals strong rights over their personal data and imposes strict obligations on organisations.

- **Entered into force**: 25 May 2018
- **Core principles**: Lawfulness, fairness, transparency, purpose limitation, data minimisation, accuracy, storage limitation, integrity & confidentiality, accountability.

## Key Rights (Chapter III)

- Right to access, rectification, erasure ("right to be forgotten"), restriction, portability, objection
- Right not to be subject to automated decision-making (including profiling)

## Key Obligations for Controllers/Processors

- Lawful basis for processing
- Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIA) for high-risk processing
- Record of processing activities (Art. 30)
- Breach notification within 72 hours (Art. 33)
- Appointment of a Data Protection Officer (DPO) where required

## Supervisory Authorities

- **EDPB** (European Data Protection Board) — https://edpb.europa.eu
- **EDPS** (European Data Protection Supervisor) — https://edps.europa.eu
- National DPAs (e.g. GBA/APD in Belgium)
